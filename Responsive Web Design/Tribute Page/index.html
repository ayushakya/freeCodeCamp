Leonardo da Vinci

The Renaissance Man who revolutionized art


The Life of Leonardo da Vinci

  • 15th April, 1452: Born in a Tuscan Hill Town of Vinci to Ser Piero da Vinci and Caterina.

  • mid 1460s: Moves to Florence, where he becomes a garzone in the workshop of Andrea del Verocchio. Becomes exposed to both theoretical training and a wide range of technical skills including drafting, chemistry, metallurgy, metal working, as well as the artistic skills of drawing, painting, sculpting, and modelling

  • 1472: Qualifies as a master in the Guild of Saint Luke, the guild of artists and doctors of medicine.

  • 1478: Receives an independent commission to paint an altarpiece for the Chapel of St. Bernard in the Palazzo Vecchio.

  • 1477-1499: Focuses on his religious subjects, but also takes portrait commissions. Notable paintings include Madonna of the Carnation, Ginevra de' Benci, Benois Madonna, Adoration of the Magi, and St. Jerome in the Wilderness.

  • 1500-1508: Returns home to Florence. Acts as military architect and engineer in the service of Cesare Borgia in 1502. Conferred the title of premier architect, artist and mechanic to the king by King Francis I of France.

  • 2 May, 1519: Dies at Clos Lucé, at the age of 67 of an apparent recurrent stroke.

  • Famous Paintings: Baptism of Christ, Saint Jerome in the Wilderness, Adoration of the Magi, Virgin of the Rocks, Lady with an Ermine, The Last Supper, Battle of Anghiari, Mona Lisa, Virgin and Child with St. Anne.

  • Notable Drawings: Vitruvian Man, Head of an Angel, The Virgin and Child with Saint Anne and Saint John the Baptist.
